Spanish peanuts are one of four main types of peanuts grown in the U.S. and are distinguishable from their other counterparts (Runner, Valencia, and Virginia) by their smaller kernels, reddish-brown skin, and higher oil content. Depending on the cultivar selected, Spanish peanuts can take 105-115 days to mature
